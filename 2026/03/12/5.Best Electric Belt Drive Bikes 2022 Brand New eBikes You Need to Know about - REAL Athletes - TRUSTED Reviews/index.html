Best Commuter Belt Drive eBike
Lekker Amsterdam GT Enviolo Belt Drive eBike
A new release in 2021. The Amsterdam GT Enviolo builds on the expertise that Lekker have amassed in belt drive bikes. The Lekker Amsterdam GT Enviolo has a single speed style about it. With an integrated battery within the frame it can easily pass for a standard bike and will appeal to many on this basis.
The Amsterdam GT is very well specced using the Gates CDX belt drive system and the lightweight Samsung 410Wh cell battery giving a range of up to 100km on a single charge. If you are looking for more power to give assurance on range and infrequency of charging there is also a 500Wh battery that can reach up to 140km range.
Power is delivered by a Max Mid-drive motor from Bafang creating a maximum power output of 80N.m. Not going to be threatening any Ferrari’s at the lights but mighty on a bike. The GT comes with gearing using the Enviolo city stepless shifting technology. This delivers a continuous variable transmission perfect for stress free commuters with enough to think about traffic dodging. Enviolo have been developing their integrated hub to allow shifting while standing still making stop-start journeys simpler.

Trek Allant+ 9S Stagger eBike
Another completely new biike from Trek Bikes is the Allant+ 9S Stagger eBike. The Allant is a bruiser of an eBike with a sturdy look about it but only weighing in at 23.6kg that is comparable with others in our list. Styling wise with a drop top tube the ambition from Trek is to achieve a unisex frame in lightweight aluminium. It may be a Marmite design decision and not convinced it will be genuinely a one size fits all in the spirit of a Brompton but a brave choice.
The Allant shares many of the components with the Lekker Amsterdam Enviolo GT above. The drive train and gearing are mirrored with the Gates CDX carbon belt drive coupled to the Enviolo CVT rear hub. Trek have adopted the Bosch motor and battery. This has one of the most powerful batteries in our list of belt drive eBikes with the Bosch motor delivering a slightly lower max power output of 75Nm. The battery capacity is a monster 625 Wh delivered in an integrated battery. This is 125 Wh larger than the upgraded Lekker model. The range of this combination gives up to 150 miles of assisted cycling but this varies dramatically depending on speed, cadence and weight.
The Allant + 9S comes with a Suntour suspension front fork with lockout for those not looking to lose any power and trade comfort for speed. The seat post includes suspension mitigating need for full suspension in a more cost effective manner. In addition to suspension the Allant has 4-piston hydraulic disc brakes for confidence in all-weather conditions. Final features include mudguards, rear rack and lights to further provision all-rounder capability.

Giant DailyTour E+1 Electric Hybrid Bike
The Giant DailyTour E+1 is so new that technically at the time of putting this list together you can’t yet buy one. This is certainly bleeding edge when it comes to belt driven eBikes. Details we have to date are as follows and will get a full review soon as we can get our hand on one.
The Giant DailyTour differs from others in our line with Giant having developed much of their own electric bike tech. They are still using the unrivalled Gates carbon belt drive system but matching this with their own battery and motors. Power comes in lower than many others with Torque of only 50Nm (Newton meters). This means electric support on hills will be lower than some others here. Important if you are a heavier rider. Range will be excellent however with a class leading voltage capacity of 625Wh (Watt Hours). This puts it head to head with the Trek.

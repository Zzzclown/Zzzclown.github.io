    What’s a Water Distiller?
    A water distiller is an electric appliance used to transform your contaminated tap water into 100% pure water through the distillation process.
    As you already know, distillation is the process of removing contaminants in water such as heavy metals, salts, through the boiling process. The water is heated up to boiling point, and the steam it produces is condensed back to liquid to become the distilled water.
    When the steam evaporates from the water, it leaves behind a solution of concentrated minerals and other contaminants.
    A typical water distiller features an electric heat element (rated 1,000 to 1,500 watts) responsible for boiling water in a stainless steel tank. The boiling process results in steam which then enters a stainless steel cooling coil—where it’s condensed to water.
    Once your home distiller has boiled out all the water, a float switch will jump in to turn it off. You can then drain off the concentrated contaminants solution and refill it manually to continue with its job.
    The best home water distiller should be able to remove more than 95% of the minerals found in water.
    These include sodium, nitrate, sulfate, and arsenic. The distillation process also destroys all the bacteria, cysts, and viruses present in water.
    Most of the units meant for home use come with a portable countertop design, connect to the 120V electrical outlet, and distill around 3-4 liters of water at a go.
    However, some models are meant for permanent installation (say in your home basement). You simply integrated these units into your water supply line to distilled all your home drinking and cooking water. The purified water usually collects in a storage container from where it’s supplied to the tap, fridges, etc., with the help of a small pump.

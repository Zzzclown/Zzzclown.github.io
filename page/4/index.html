60 Best Dinner Ideas
dinner round up
We’re rounding up our 60 best dinner ideas.
Posted May 23, 2021 by Stephanie
We’re rounding up our 60 best dinner ideas! We’ve got everything from super easy pastas to slow cooked tender taco fillings – we’ve got you covered when you’re wondering: what should I cook for dinner tonight?
It’s the eternal question: what’s should I make for dinner tonight? It’s always hard to balance taste, easy, and heath. Plus when there are just SO many ideas out there, how do you decide? I have the worst indecision ever so I put together this lil round up of our 60 best dinner ideas. Now, all you have to do is pick one!
Pin
Our best chicken dinner ideas
There’s a reason why chicken is the most popular protein. Chicken is flavorful, fast, and makes the best dinners.
Lemon Pepper Chicken and Cous Cous. Freshly squeezed lemon juice and zest with poppy pepper adds a huge amount of flavor in this Greek inspired dish.
Teriyaki Chicken. There’s a reason why teriyaki chicken is a perennial favorite: sweet and savory juicy chicken with loads of sauce, perfect for serving up with rice and veggies.
One Pot Hainanese Chicken Rice. Possibly the best comfort food ever: chicken and rice cooked up with a trio of aromatics. So simple but so, so good.
Malaysian Coconut Chicken Curry. Rich, spicy, and full of coconut flavors, juicy chicken, and tender chunks of potato.
Thai Basil Chicken. You can make this with ground chicken or chicken thighs – either will taste amazing. This is a super quick and flavor forward stir fry that will save you $$ on take out.
One Pan Dijon Mushroom Chicken. One pan, chicken, mushrooms, dijon, kale, all the good stuff!
Pin
Our best beef dinner ideas
There’s something so satisfying about beef for dinner, especially if it’s a special occasion.f
Beef Tenderloin. Tenderloin might be the ultimate in luxuriousness. Here’s everything you need to know so you don’t mess up.
Steak Frites with 8 Different Kinds of Steak Sauces. If you want the ultimate steak house experience at home, make one of these 8 steak sauces and serve up your steak with perfectly crispy fries. Pro tip, check out this post on how to cook steak.
Tomahawk Steak. Why go home when you’re already at home? Go big instead and treat yourself to a tomahawk steak. Everything you need to know about tempering, temperature, and how to cook your big bone in steak without ruining it.
Vietnamese Lemongrass Beef Bowl. Thinly sliced beef marinated in a sweet and spicy marinade, then fried to smoky perfection and served over rice or vermicelli and doused in fish sauce.
Osso Buco. It doesn’t get better than rich and meaty melt in your mouth beef shank in a bone marrow tomato sauce.
Bulgogi. Thin and tender sliced meat marinated in a sweet and spicy sauce, just right for eating in rice bowls or alongside a salad if you’re feeling healthy.
Pin
Our best pork dinner ideas
Pork the one you love – that’s an old school pork slogan and I LOVE it. It cooks fast, it’s juicy, and it’s super filling.
Pork Tenderloin. Tenderloins are a great dinner choice. The cook up quickly and become melt in your mouth pieces of awesomeness, especially drenched in a lemon rosemary, fennel sauce.
Easy Chinese Char Siu. I LOVE a big bowl of rice topped off with char siu and the best part is that it’s so much easier to make than you think. Marinate, roast, slice, and you’re in rice bowl heaven.
Air Fryer Pork Chops. These air fryer pork chops are one and done. The herby green sauce is full of fresh rosemary, crushed fennel seeds, lemon, and parsley.
Oven Baked Tonkatsu. All the crispy crunch of Japanese tonkatsu without the deep frying so it’s healthier. Serve this up with rice and a big side of shredded cabbage.
Larb Moo Pork. Ground pork is the star of this stir fry featuring fish sauce, shallots, mint, and cilantro. Eat it wrapped up in little lettuce cups with rice.
Honey Garlic Ribs. Hands off oven baked honey garlic pork ribs: crispy, sweet, savory, and super addictive.
Pin
Our best pasta dinner ideas
For those days when nothing but carbs will do, these are our best pastas. Comfort food at its best!
Brown Butter Parmesan Noodles. These are just like the butter noodles you loved as a kid, but all grown up with nutty brown butter, loads of garlic, and parm.
Vodka Sauce. Why just drink vodka when you can have it in your pasta sauce too? Creamy, tomato-y, and fancy enough for date night or easy enough for a quick weeknight dinner.
Velveeta Mac and Cheese. Classic for a reason, this is THE mac and cheese recipe you’ve been searching for. Super creamy, cheesy, and full of noodles.
Creamy Cacio e Pepe. Extra creamy cacio e pepe that’s full of cheese and pepper and a not so traditional ingredient: cream.
Ragu di Prosciutto. Everyone loves Bolognese sauce but what about Modenese sauce? This sauce is from an area just a bit North of Bologna and is made with ground pork, prosciutto, and mortadella.
Weeknight Pasta Tips. Here’s everything you’ll ever need to know about making the greatest weeknight pasta of your life. Grab some fridge ingredients, read these tips and cozy up to a plate of deliciousness.

Our best noodle dinner ideas
Do you love noodles? We do too, we are totally on board for that noodle life. Slurp up because these noodle bowls for dinner are going to give you life.
Zha Jiang Mian. A super umami forward sweet and savory meat sauce that tastes amazing with noodles.
Beef and Broccoli Chow Mein. Forget ordering takeout and make beef and broccoli chow mein at home.
Pork Belly and Kale Fried Noodles. Crispy pork belly and hearty kale in a sweet and smoky noodle dish.
Yaki Udon. Thick and chewy udon noodles with pork and cabbage, just like the kind you get at a Japanese summer festival.
Khao Soi Coconut Curry Chicken Noodle Soup. Deliciously warming coconut curry noodles with chicken, lime, and crunchy wonton crisps.
Mayo Ramen. Don’t knock it til you try it: an egg yolk and mayo mixed into your ramen broth gives you a luxurious creamy bowl that’s restaurant level.

Our best Instant Pot dinner recipes
Set it and forget it with these Instant Pot dinner ideas. Pressure cookers are a dinner life saver.
Instant Pot Chicken Alfredo. A classic favorite made quick and easy in the Instant Pot.
Instant Pot Chili. This is a dinner time favorite, especially if you have a toppings bar with shredded cheese, fritos, and sour cream.
Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie Pasta. When you want chicken pot pie flavors but you don’t want to roll out pie crust, this pot pie pasta is here to save the day.
Instant Pot Pasta and Meatballs. Put everything in the Instant Pot, including the meatballs and raw pasta, and turn it on for al dente noodles and meatballs in a tangy rich tomato sauce.
Instant Pot Butter Chicken. Creamy, saucy, tomato butter chicken that’s just begging for naan and rice.
Instant Pot Ginger Chicken and Rice Soup. Light and gingery with tender bits of rice, carrots, and mushrooms in a soy sauce seasoned broth, done in a flash.

Chicken Tinga. Chunks of chicken in a tomato chipotle sauce: sweet and smoky and savory. We love this in tacos and also just with Mexican rice.
Chicken Enchiladas. Juicy chicken tucked into tortillas and smothered in enchilada sauce and cheese.
Barbacoa. Juicy, soft, tender spiced beef perfect for tacos, rice plates, or burritos.

Al Pastor. Sweet and smoky thanks to chipotle in adobo al pastor is perfect with roasted pineapples tucked into tortillas.
Birria Tacos. Tender, fall apart beef inside a crisp taco served with spicy consome for dipping.

Need Dinner Ideas?
Need Dinner Ideas?
Do you need dinner ideas? I don’t know about you but I get tired of making the same thing night after night so I’m gonna try to make something different every night. Sometimes it may just be making something using the leftovers from the night before and other nights it might be somethin’ totally different.
Hey…maybe you can help me figure out what to make for dinner too sometimes! Just submit them below and I’ll be happy to add your ideas for dinner too!
Also see: What’s for Dinner Page 2 for more dinner ideas!
I love when the weather gets warm enough to get out the grill. Tonight I made Grilled Cornish Hens, dirty rice (you can find the recipe in my ecookbook) and grilled asparagus.
Here’s a meal a bit on the lighter side. Oven Fried Cod Fish, with an easy rice. I put a cup of white rice in a saucepan, added a can of chicken broth, brought it to a boil, reduced to a simmer and covered and simmered until the all of the liquid was absorbed (about 20 minutes). I then added a can of Chicken with rice soup and mixed well. Added a side of peas for a complete, satisfying meal!
I love soup and sandwiches when I’m want something a little different for dinner. I made chicken salad sandwiches to go with some tomato basil soup.
Grilled Teriyaki wings with macaroni and cheese and steamed broccoli is a dinner everyone will love!
This Melt-in-your-mouth Salisbury Steak (you can find the recipe in my ecookbook), mashed potatoes and roasted Brussels sprouts is a wonderful supper idea on a crisp fall day!
If you are looking for new dinner ideas for chicken that everyone will love, this Loaded Chicken (the recipe is in my ecookbook) may be just what you are looking for. It has a wonderful sauce and the chicken turns out moist and tender every time! I served it with mashed potatoes and fresh green beans that I sauteed in butter, onions and a little salt and pepper.
So, how about this marinated then grilled salmon, with baked potatoes and a salad.
I used my pressure cooker to make this wonderful Pot Roast but you can certainly use a slow cooker or even cook it on top of the stove. I served this with cornbread to sop up all of that wonderful gravy!
Looking for comforting dinner ideas? This Chicken and Mashed Potato Casserole is kinda like a cross between a Shepperd’s Pie and a Chicken Pot Pie.
Need quick dinner ideas? I just picked up some bread dough from the bakery at the local grocery store and got out the grill! I topped this grilled pizza with some canadian bacon, mozzarella cheese, green peppers, onions, mushrooms, black olives and cherry tomatoes. I partially cooked the peppers, onions and mushrooms in a saute pan on the stove before topping the pizza. This meal literally takes just a few minutes to cook so be sure to have you toppings ready and standing by.
I started out to make a light dinner of a Baked Potato and a Chef Salad. The problem was, I kept adding things to the salad. I started out with romaine lettuce, purple cabbage, carrots, green peppers, celery and tomatoes and then added grilled chicken, bacon, boiled eggs, a blend of cheeses, croutons…oh well…my intentions were good.
This meal may take a little time to make but in the end, it’s well worth the effort. Smothered Pork Chops with mashed potatoes (I used unpeeled red skin potatoes), greens, pinto beans(the recipe is in my ecookbook) and Cornbread muffins .
You’ve heard of beer can chicken…how about Root beer can chicken? This turned out very moist and delicious! I served it with parsley potatoes (you can find the recipe in my ecookbook )and green beans.
How about hamburgers cooked on the grill? I added some Heinz 57 steak sauce, Worcestershire sauce, an egg, a couple of tablespoons of bread crumbs, garlic powder, salt and pepper to the ground beef and then formed it into patties and grilled. With it I made hashbrown casserole and corn on the cob.
Rib Eye Steak with southern potato salad on a bed of Romaine Lettuce with cherry tomatoes and mushrooms sauteed in butter!
Why not make individual Chicken Cornbread Casseroles (the recipe is in my ecookbook)! I served these with a mess ‘o greens and cornbread !
I took my meatloaf recipe, formed it into a loaf and placed it on a piece of heavy duty aluminum foil. I put it on the grill and grilled it until it reached an internal temperature of 160 degrees F which took about 1 1/2 hours. I put the tomato topping on after an hour. With it I made mashed potatoes with a beefy mushroom gravy and broccoli. It turned out really good!
Here’s a great dinner idea….how about breakfast for dinner! How about an omelet! For this one, I sauteed some fresh mushrooms in a little butter with a some diced onion. Once the onions were soft, I added some fresh spinach and cooked it just long enough to wilt the spinach. I stuffed the omelet with the spinach mixture along with some bacon that had been cooked crisp and some Monterrey Jack cheese. I served it with hashbrowns and some fresh tomatoes out of the garden. It really hit the spot!
This is an oven fried chicken recipe using ranch dressing mix. I served it with Noodles and Broccoli and a side salad. An easy dinner with a lot of flavor!
This is one of those 30 minute meals. Homemade Sloppy Joes with buffalo potato wedges (you’ll find the recipe in my ecookbook) and a tossed salad.
I don’t know about you but sometimes, I just get a hankerin’ for vegetables. Sometimes we just have vegetable plates for dinner. Here, we had pinto beans (the recipe is in my ecookbook ), hash brown casserole, a mess o’ greens, fried okra, corn on the, sliced tomatoes straight from the garden and, of course, cornbread. It really hit the spot!
Tonight was Barbeque Shrimp over white rice, and baby Lima beans that I seasoned with a little bacon drippings.
Looking for quick dinner ideas? I made this easy hamburger stroganoff in less than 30 minutes and everyone was raving about how great it was. I served it with peas for a quick dinner.
I used penne pasta in place of spaghetti for something a little different. I served it with a side salad and some Sister Shubert Rolls (you can get them in the freezer section of the grocery store)

10 Best Games For Lord Of The Rings Fans (That Aren’t Based On The Movies)
Lord of the Rings fans that are looking for some exciting games with epic themes of adventure can check out these 10 great options influenced by LOTR.
By Zach Gass Published Feb 16, 2021
J.R.R. Tolkien’s The Lord of the Rings is the epitome of the fantasy epic. The saga of the One Ring has been a pillar of the fantasy genre since it was first published, inspiring other fantastical adventures across other mediums such as film, television, and video games.

While there have been several titles that pull directly from the film adaptations from Peter Jackson, such as War in the North and Shadow of Mordor, the Ring of Power’s influence is also felt across other virtual adventures as well, with and without Hobbits.
10 The Hobbit
This video game adaptation of Tolkien’s beloved prequel gets a spot on the list because instead of taking from Peter Jackson’s films, it takes its influence right from the source material, with a little Zelda thrown in for good measure. It’s essentially a Zelda-like game with a Middle Earth skin, but it’s great for new gamers.
It plays like a “my-first- Zelda game,” in which players take up the role of Bilbo Baggins and hack and slash their way through chapters of the novel from “An Unexpected Party” to “The Return Journey.”
9 The Legend Of Zelda: Breath Of The Wild
On to a real adventure: a pointy-eared protagonist must take up a magic sword to save races of people from an evil darkness that lives in a foreboding tower. Does any of this sound familiar?
RELATED: Top 5 Legend Of Zelda Minigames
Although Middle Earth might lack Hylians and Gorons, the breathtaking mountains, orcish minions, and enchanted weapons would definitely feel right at home in Tolkien’s masterpiece. The game could definitely use more wizards and eagles, though.
8 Final Fantasy
Any RPG that has the player lead a party of adventurers is bound to have Middle Earth’s influence. Enter the original Final Fantasy game, with a quest that changed the gaming industry as players knew it.
The premise is simple but definitely one that Tolkien would approve of. While the crystals might not be as powerful as the magic rings, there is a certain familiarity to seeing the hero characters wield them before embarking on their adventure.
7 Dragon’s Crown
Dragon’s Crown is one of the most beautiful fantasy games ever created. It as if all the best of the best of the genre came together in one glorious hand-drawn adventure for gamers to enjoy, and Lord of the Rings is, unquestionably, a heavy influence.
RELATED: 10 Games To Play If You Like Diablo
Swords, sorcery, elves, dwarves, and a golden artifact that grants its wearer phenomenal power are all trademarks of Tolkien’s work. The real question is how can one not think of Middle Earth when playing this game?
6 Kingdoms Of Amalur: Re-Reckoning
Kingdoms of Amalur might take more from games like Skyrim and World of Warcraft, but the war between the Fae and the mortal races of Amalur feels vaguely similar to the battle for Middle Earth. Granted, it would have been a lot shorter if Frodo were as powerful as the Fateless One.
From its high-fantasy visuals to its massive sword-swinging battles, Amalur will satisfy anyone with a remote interest in the genre, including those baptized in the waters of Rivendell.
5 Skyrim
Just as one does not simply walk into Mordor, one does not simply talk about fantasy RPGs without talking about Skyrim . Tolkien pulled a lot of his inspiration from Norse mythology, as did Skyrim.

Characters like Gimli, Boromir, and Aragorn seem like they’d be just a little bit too comfortable in the mountains of Tamriel. The title is certainly more dragon-heavy than any of the books or movies, but it’s impossible not to think of Erebor or Moria when exploring some of the dungeons.
4 Divinity: Original Sin Series
There are plenty of isometric RPGs that take influence from Tolkien’s work, but one of the more recent entries in the genre that fits this description is Divinity: Original Sin. What LOTR does better than many stories it has influenced is its world-building and how Middle Earth is affected by the characters.
This rich RPG experience has incredible lore, characters, and a world that would rival many of Tolkien’s imaginings. It’s a perfect game for those wanting to lose themselves in the fantasy.
3 Dragon Age Series
Of course, one can’t mention a story-heavy RPG without mentioning something by BioWare. The Dragon Age series is absolutely ideal for those who want to experience a rich story with familiar motifs and visuals from the fantasy genre.
RELATED: Best Mage Builds in Dragon Age: Inquisition
Dragon Age wears its Tolkien influence on its sleeves, especially when considering the cultures of the different races. While Darkspawn might be a tougher order than the average army of orcs, the races of men, elves, and dwarves must stand together when their realm is threatened. Certainly sounds like a LOTR plot.
2 Dragon’s Dogma
For those looking for something more action-heavy, the answer lies on a golden platter with Dragon’s Dogma . While it might rely on more traditional fantasy tropes rather than Tolkien’s alone, the feeling of journeying with a party of adventurers simply drips with Fellowship of the Ring every time.
Tradition is the name of the game in this adventure, and while its titular dragon is a tad more demonic, there are elements of Smaug the Terrible in his presentation. It certainly feels familiar, at any rate.
1 Dragon Quest XI
The likes of Final Fantasy might have pulled from Eastern and classic mythology, but the Dragon Quest series unashamedly pulled from the West in its influences, especially from The Lord of the Rings. But nowhere is this more prominently felt than in Dragon Quest IX.
A chosen one that leads a party of adventurers across a massive fantasy landscape to defeat a dark lord? It really doesn’t get more direct than that. While it lacks certain choices from Tolkien’s work, few capture its undiluted spirit of adventure.
